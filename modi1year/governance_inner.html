Including the poorest in banking economy

125 million bank accounts opened in six months under Jan Dhan scheme

The Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana, which aims to provide every Indian family with a bank account is already, is already in its second rollout phase.

The financial inclusion plan, which has been launched in two phases, provides the poor with zero balance bank accounts with an inbuilt insurance cover of Rs 1 lakh.

Prime Minister Modi has asked the Reserve Bank of India to prepare a 20-year road map for financial inclusion and urged banks to be considerate while giving loans and while making recoveries from the poor, especially the farmers.

PM Modi has rightly pointed out that the success of the scheme has shown the potential for the enormous role the banking sector can play in ensuring financial inclusion. The initiative has been lauded not just by the captains of industry but also the World Bank. A recent World Bank report pointed out that between August 2014 and January 2015, 125 million bank accounts had been opened under the Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana.
